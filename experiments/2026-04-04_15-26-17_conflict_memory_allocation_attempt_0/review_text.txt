    "Summary": "This paper studies a lightweight feedback mechanism for decoupled multi-agent scheduling called Conflict Memory Allocation (CMA). After a downstream path planner resolves conflicts, observed conflicts are projected onto a spatial grid and accumulated via an exponential moving average. This learned conflict memory is then used as an additive soft penalty on surrogate single-agent routes during future task allocation. The paper is framed as a negative-result study: the authors report that the learned memory map becomes sparse and structured, with persistent hotspots aligned with bottlenecks, but that this learned signal yields little downstream benefit in the retained evaluation artifact. The main reported quantitative anchors are a best penalty weight of 0.5 and a labeled final improvement of only 0.0123, alongside a per-instance plot showing mostly near-zero improvement ratios and one substantial negative outlier.",
